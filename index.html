Malaysia's inflation rate has fluctuated over the years.
Inflation reflects the rate at which prices for goods and services rise.

Peaks occurred around 2017 and 2022.
Significant dip into negative inflation was observed in 2020 during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Inflation Rates across various economic sectors in 2022 and 2023

The highest inflation rates (5-6%) were observed in Communication, Restaurants & Hotels, and Food & Beverages.
Household Equipment was the only category showing negative inflation.
